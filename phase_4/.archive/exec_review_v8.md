### Issues Found
- [HIGH] Google canceled subscriptions lose paid access before they actually expire - `src/modules/billing/google-play.ts:142-145`, `src/modules/entitlements/service.ts:16-31`, `src/modules/entitlements/service.ts:87-127`, `src/modules/entitlements/service.ts:155-166` - `SUBSCRIPTION_STATE_CANCELED` is mapped to internal `CANCELED`, and the entitlement engine immediately converts `CANCELED` to `expired/inactive` even when `current_period_end` is still in the future. I reproduced this through the live verify route and unified reads: verifying a Google subscription with `status: "CANCELED"` and `current_period_end: "2026-04-01T00:00:00.000Z"` returned `entitlement_code: "expired"` and `status: "inactive"` right away. That contradicts Google's subscription-cancel semantics and will revoke valid Android paid access early. Fix by treating canceled subscriptions as still entitling until their current period actually ends, while preserving cancellation metadata for UI and provider sync. Verify by adding integration coverage for verify, restore, and RTDN sync on a canceled subscription whose expiry is in the future and asserting that unified entitlement remains paid until expiry, then drops only after the period ends or the provider reports expiry.
- [HIGH] Claimed Google acknowledgment retries can become permanently stranded after a crash - `src/modules/billing/repository.ts:326-372`, `src/modules/billing/google-play-support.ts:164-191`, `src/modules/billing/google-play-support.ts:460-490` - the retry scanner only finds rows whose `acknowledgment_next_retry_at` is due, but `claimPurchaseTokenAcknowledgmentRetry()` changes the row to `PENDING` and clears `acknowledgment_next_retry_at` before the network call. If the process dies after claim and before success/failure persistence, the row is no longer due, has no stale-lock recovery, and will never be retried. I reproduced this by forcing an initial ack failure, manually making the row due, claiming it with the live repository, and then running `runWebhookRetryJob()`: the job scanned zero items and the token stayed stuck in `PENDING` with `acknowledgmentNextRetryAt: null`. Fix by making acknowledgment retries use durable claim metadata with stale recovery, or by preserving a reclaimable due time until the acknowledge attempt finishes, so crashes cannot strand the row. Verify by simulating a crash after claim, rerunning the retry job after the reclaim window, and asserting that the token is processed or rescheduled instead of staying stuck forever.
